Scratching or Scurrying Sounds in Walls or Attic

One of the most common and undeniable signs of a squirrel presence within your property is the distinct sounds of movement from within your walls or attic. These aren't the quiet rustles you might attribute to settled dust or creaking pipes; rather, they are often loud and persistent scratching, scurrying, and sometimes even gnawing noises, particularly noticeable during the early morning and late afternoon hours when squirrels are most active. These sounds indicate that squirrels are actively navigating through these spaces, likely establishing or maintaining a nest. The sounds can originate from various locations within the structure, seemingly moving as the squirrel explores or works. Hearing these noises is a strong indicator that squirrels have successfully breached the exterior of your home and are making themselves comfortable in areas not intended for wildlife. Ignoring these sounds in hopes that the problem will resolve itself is ill-advised. The longer squirrels inhabit these hidden spaces, the more likely they are to cause significant damage and leave behind unsanitary waste. Prompt investigation and intervention by a professional are crucial upon hearing such sounds.
